Step 1: Decide on a Solution

Before settling on a solution, it‚Äôs crucial to fully understand the problem. As a product manager, I did this by getting as close to the users as possible. This meant observing their processes, conducting open-ended interviews, and synthesizing my findings. This helped my team to stay focused on the user's pain points and make sure they felt heard.

Step 2: Design and Build the Solution

It can be difficult to turn a great idea into a real product without losing something in translation. In the past, I have worked to accurately assess the goals of various stakeholders, translate them into simple design patterns, visualize these patterns with wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes, and work side-by-side with engineering as they were developed. This ensured that everyone stayed on the same page.

Primary skills: Design patterns, quality assurance, typography, design software (Figma, Invision, Adobe XD)
